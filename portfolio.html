PLTW 1.1.9- The Snowglobe



Using Python's turtle feature on Visual Studies, my group and I had designed a neat snowglobe fit for a Christmas night. A snowglobe that could be gifted to loved ones, for them to stare deep into a cute and cozy home owned by two lovely snowmen. Here, they're celebrating the holidays as well, with a cute little while-loop-based wreath that mesmorizes others as the lights light up.

PLTW 1.2.5- Fly High in the Sky



For the second project, we created a game where the objective is to fuel a jet via mid-air refueling. We initialized a gas pump, timer, and plane, and then created the game functions which would house the key components of our game. “Refilling” the gasoline of the jet would change its location as well as the gas pump(once it collided with the plane). In addition, the jet colliding with a bird results in the loss of the game simply because killing birds is bad. Finally, the score counter holds how many points the player has, and the timer indicates how much time the player had before the turtle would hide and the death screen would appear. Consisting of multiple levels, this game is fun to play whenever you may be bored and want to get competitive.

PLTW 1.3- Fireboy!



For this project, we decided to create a game that is similar to the popular childhood skills game "Fireboy and Watergirl." However, our version consists of Fireboy going out on his very own timed journey, perhaps an identity journey with the fire platforms hurting him rather than aiding him. In order for this to occur, fireboy was given on-key "W A D" movements, and collision detection was inluded between fireboy and the randomized "bad" platforms. In addition to this, randomized diamonds could be collected as fireboy traveled through the mysterious temple that would give him a letter grade upon his exit through the gates.

Scratch- Papa's Pizzeria



We developed a nostalgic Papa's game for our first Scratch project! In it, you take a customer's randomly generated pizza order and design it as accurately as possible. There are multiple stations in which you may do so, such as the topping of the pizza, baking of it until cooked properly, and cutting of it into equal slices for the customers. The customer will then respond to you and grade the pizza based on how well you followed their order. In addition, there are multiple difficulties in case the user would like to challenge themself!
